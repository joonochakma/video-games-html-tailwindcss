Playstation

Quick history of Playstation

  • Playstation 1 (PS1): It was among the first wave of consoles capable of delivering 3D visuals, while also being arguebly the first CD-based console to realy gain a widespread, global phenomenon. CDs had much slower loading timesthan cartridges but offered 700MB of data compared to the maximum storage of 64GB seen in many N64 games. This allows PS1 developers to cran tons of in-game content , plenty of hours of audio tracking/voice acting, and loads of video game content into their games.
  • Playstation 2 (PS2): Released in 2000, Sony embraced the multimedia experience by offering support to DVDs. Emotion Engine CPU CPU and Graphics Synthesizer CPU required plenty of finese on the parts of developers. Studios in the 2000s were able to deliver gorgeous games like God Of War titles, Metal Gear Solid 2, Devil May Cry 3 and more.....
  • Playstation 3 (PS3): The PS3 was filled to the brim with exotic and/or cutting-edge tech at launch, such as a purpose-built Cell processor, NVIDIA RSX GPU, and a Blu-Ray drive. And this was Sony’s first console designed with HD from the get-go, supporting an HDMI output and 1080p resolution. The company even offered support for PS2 games by including the PS2’s hardware in the new console. Unfortunately for Sony, the first year and a bit of release was a disappointment due to both the price and the somewhat barren game library. This period even saw the spawning of the “PS3 has no games” meme, owing to the drought of titles in the early going.
  • Playstation 4 (PS4): It seemed like Sony learned its lesson with the PS3, as 2013’s PS4 launched at a solid $399 price tag and opted for largely conventional hardware. The PS4 offered an AMD APU, featuring an octa-core Jaguar-based CPU, custom AMD graphics, and 8GB of GDDR5 RAM. This architecture made the PS4 a very developer-friendly console, allowing studios to hit the ground running and deliver some great titles from the get-go. These games included exclusives like Uncharted 4: A Thief’s End, Horizon Zero Dawn, God of War, Marvel’s Spider-Man, and Ghost of Tsushima. The vast majority of multi-platform games also ran better on the PS4 compared to the Xbox One.
  • Playstation 5 (PS5): Sony’s latest console is 2020’s PlayStation 5, and it represented another major step forward in terms of technology. The Achilles heel of the PS4 was the CPU, which was actually built with low-end tablets and netbooks in mind. But Sony upgraded things in a big way with the PS5, using an octa-core AMD Zen 2 CPU paired with RDNA2 graphics and 16GB of GDDR6 RAM. Perhaps the biggest upgrade comes in the storage arena, as the PS5 adopts a super-fast custom NVMe M.2 SSD (825GB). This enables lightning-fast loading times in games, even in open-world titles. Sony has also focused on audio with the new console, with its so-called Tempest Engine tech enabling more immersive audio effects when used with headphones.

Recent trends & developments

Since Playstation’s target audience viewed as gamers or casual gamers who are depicted as between 18-25 years old and 26-29 years old, Playstation has developed controllers headsets, video games, charging stations and storage cases for latest technology, including the latest PS5 which advances the entertainment, socialization and relaxation for satisfied audiences.

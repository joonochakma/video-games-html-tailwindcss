PC gaming

Quick history of PC gaming

  • The 1970s: The earliest computer games were developed in the 1970s and they were text-based adventures and simple games. Even though it was not played on devices we would recognise as personal computers, gaming may never have evolved in the same way if Allan Alcorn had not created Pong in 1972, a super popular tennis-style game involved two players using sliding paddles in a battle to score 11 points and was instrumental in the success of Atari and the advent of at-home gaming in the years to come.
  • The 1980s: The introduction of personal computers in the 1980s. such as the IBM PC and Apple II, led to a boom in computer gaming One of the most notable early releases for the IBM PC was Microsoft Flight Simulator, which pushed the limits of the computer's processor. memory, and graphics as the more recent versions still do today. Popular games during this time included adventures games like King's Quest and Space Quest.
  • The 1990s: The 1990s saw the rise of 3D graphics and the introduction of new genres of PC games, including first-person shooters like Doom and Quake. there were two notable FFS games that established a formula for success for modern-day shooters, along with real-time strategy games like Warcraft and Command & Conquer, and massively multiplayer online games like Ultima Online and EverQuest.
  • The 2000s: The first few decades of the 2000s have seen spectacular developments in the consumer PC hardware market, with Intel, AMD, and Nvidia rapidly increasing the processing and graphical power of personal computers to accompany high-resolution flat-screen monitors. Gaming PCs built with the components and physical traits that are designed to separate themselves from other computers have become increasingly popular. Steam, first released in 2003, has exploded in popularity as a centralized library and marketplace of PC games with over 50,000 titles, including vintage games highlighting the best titles in the history of PC games. The future of PC gaming has never been brighter, with virtual reality and other innovations making a new level of immersion possible. Popular games during this time included Half-Life 2 Warcraft, and The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion, which cement the history of PC games.
  • The 2010s and beyond: The past decade has seen continued growth in PC gaming, with the rise of esports and the continued popularity of online gaming. PC games now offer even more sophisticated graphics and gameplay, and virtual reality technologies has opened up new possibilities for immersive gaming experience.

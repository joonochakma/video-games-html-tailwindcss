Mobile Devices

Quick history of Mobile Devices

  • Early mobile games: Even though it was in monochrome, games like Snake, Tic-Tac-Toe and Tetris were games lmited by the technology of the time, with simple graphics and limited gameplay.
  • Rise of smartphone: The introduction of smartphones such as Iphone, Samsung, Nokia, etc... in the late 2000s and early 2010s revolutionised mobile gaming. These devices provide larger screeens, more powerful proccessors, and advanced gaming capabilities, which allowed for more complex and immersive games such as Angry Birds, Fruit Ninja and Temple Run.
  • Freemium and in-app purchases: One of the biggest trends in mobile gaming has been the rise of freemium games, which are free to download and play but include in-app purchases for items, power-ups, or other content. This business model has allowed developers to make money from their games while still offering them for free, and it has become a common practice in mobile gaming.
  • Mobile gaming today: Today, mobile gaming is a massive industry, with millions of players worldwide and a huge variety of games available. Mobile games range from simple puzzle games to complex RPGs, and they can be played on a variety of devices, including smartphones, tablets, and even smartwatches.

Recent trends & developments

Offers the opportunity to play games nearly everywhere. Networked games allows individual players to play and interact with other players and to participate in a larger gaming world, hence, a sudden increased interest for game developers, providers and players in mobile games.

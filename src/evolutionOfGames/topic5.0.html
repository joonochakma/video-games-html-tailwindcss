Evolution of Games

Timeline
of Games

What is this page about?

This page will cover the historical context of how video games evolved. We look at everything from the first video games ever made on a massive computer to the latest video game on a mobile phone. The timeline we will look at is from the 1940s to the 2020s. 

1940s

In this era, computers were very large (the size of a room). Computers were used by the military to enhance their combat, as computers could generate thousands of solutions each second. Although the computers were so big and expensive, few games were invented, such as Nimrod, OXO (tic-tac-toe), tennis for two, and Spacewars! Now you might be wondering why the video game Pong isn't here. But we'll get to that later. Although these computers have the ability to create and compute video games, only scientists and military personnel have access to them due to their size and cost. The ENIAC computer cost the government $400,000.

1960s-1970s

Computers grew in size during this period, from the size of a room to the size of a refrigerator. This allowed other universities to own more computers because of its size. However, they were still expensive, with the PDP-1 costing around $120,000. A team at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) used the PDP-1 to create a new computer game called SpaceWar! The game SpaceWar also inspired the creator of Atari, for which he created the famous game Pong. Although there has been discussion that Pong was the first computer game, Pong was the first commercial computer game, selling around 150,000 units. Even though Pong is a video game. Pong didn't use any programming language and completely relied on electrical hardware, such as digital logic gates. This was different from the video game that inspired Nolan, such as Spacewar!, that was actually developed in Assembly, a type of low-level programming language that is intended to communicate directly with a computer's hardware. 

1970s-1980s

During the late 1970s or early 1980s, video games were starting to become more popular. With arcade machines only costing around $2000 to around $20,000, which is still less than the 1960s price of computers, With computers becoming cheaper, more computers are becoming more powerful, and more arcade machines have the ability to produce colour as well as better animation, sprite interaction, and more functionality such as a score counter and a high score memory count. This paved the way for another popular game, Pac-Man, and Tetris. This also created an evolution of arcade games that kids and teens can go to at any arcade entertainment centre and insert coins to play.

1980s-1990s

During the 1980s, technology was getting better. The invention of portable phones like the Motorola MicroTAC, computers such as the Apple Macintosh 128k, and a portable radio/music player, the Sony Walkman, however,  these types of technologies very expensive. Motorola phones would be equivalent to paying $7000 or $5000 for the Apple Macintosh 128K. But the 1980s introduced a new type of technology that changed the video game industry: home consoles. Even though IBM computers have a few games and even dial-up motherboards that allow you to play games online, they can't beat the invention of the home consoles in the 1980s. The invention of home consoles allowed kids and teens to play video games at home by connecting the console to their TV and using a controller to control their characters in the game. This replaced the experience of going to an arcade machine site and inserting coins to play, resulting in businesses dying. However, new arcade machines are using new technology to improve the gaming experience, such as scan-to-play, which uses cards that have NFC technology (the same technology as credit cards) to replace the action of inserting coins into the machines.

Famous console brands such as Nintendo, Sega, and Atari dominated the video games industry by implementing a system where the user could play more than one game on their consoles. Players can buy ROM cartridges that include a game and play it from their console. This allowed players to buy multiple games and play them at home.

During the 1980s, technology was getting much better, with home consoles the size of DVD players and personal computers small enough for customers to buy and keep at home. Home consoles were just getting better with their colourful, fast-paced animation graphics and the classic parallax background effect. Though the consoles were not the only ones that were getting better, video games were getting better thanks to developers implementing better programming languages such as C++. Just look at this gameplay of OutRun Longplay.

1990s

During the 1990s, more technology advanced compared to the arcade machines of the 1970s. The internet is a new technology that has advanced video games to connect to other people around the world and the technology to build modern technology smaller and smaller, like cellphones. Video game technology was becoming more powerful and compatible with different platforms, such as PCs and home consoles, that could run 3D games with fast and amazing graphics, but it got better with another innovation: portable game players such as Nintendo Game Boys. Players can play video games without the need for an external power source or cables, making them portable to take anywhere they go while maintaining an enjoyable gaming experience. 

1990s>2000s

In the 2000s, technology innovation improved with CDs and DVDs that replaced the use of floppy discs, which allowed video games to be played on different platforms, such as consoles and personal computers, only using one certain optical medium. This era also introduces new console brands that eventually take over the previous leaders, such as Xbox and Playstation, but Nintendo also innovated their game console with movement, which was the Nintendo Wii. The technology advancement on the Nintendo Wii allows players to interact with video games with motion by using an accelerometer and gyroscope technology-based controller that creates a unique genre of games such as Wii Sports, in which players can play boxing, baseball, tennis, golf, and bowling by creating movements based in real life.

Console and PC gaming are not the only ones that contribute to the video game industry. Websites also play an important role in video games. In the early 2000s, Java and Adobe Flash sparked new ideas of games on the internet that people could play without the need of downloading; these websites were making money by showcasing ads, which also introduced internet advertisements. 

During the 2000s, many portable game players were introduced, such as the Nintendo DS and 3DS. But this also led to some mobile phones having video games. 

The Internet also changed the video game industry by creating an experience where multiple users from around the world can play on the server, cooperating or fighting each other depending on the type of game. When talking about the technology in just games, games were incorporating better fluid animation compared to the 8-bit games in the 80s and the introduction of physics engines that enhanced the collisions and object interaction in the game. 

2010s

In the 2010s, video games became even more popular due to mobile phones and their multitouch technology. which allows users to interact with their phones by their finger or hand movements Users can swipe, pinch, zoom, and draw unique patterns. This allows for the creation of new types of genres, just like the Nintendo Wii's innovations. 

With new technology in video games, higher graphics were getting better as cathode ray tube-based display units had begun to phase out in the 2000s and flat-screen TVs were becoming cheaper with high resolution and a higher refresh rate, as well as new hardware like HDMI cables that support 4K content. Home consoles could showcase other music and media streaming platforms, such as Spotify or Netflix. Games companies could also find a new way to gather money by implementing transactions in their video games by using payment APIs that are secured over the internet. This means that players can gain cool items in the game. Steam: Steam is the biggest game distribution service. That works by selling games digitally to PCs instead of on DVDs or other disc media. This will fix the problem for people with damaged or lost discs or those waiting for backorders. All users have to do is download the game. This helped big game companies cut or lower the cost to manufacture games on the medium, which is still present on other gaming platforms. 

Another advancement in video games is cross-platform gaming. This allows users to play games with other friends no matter which device or platform they are using. This works because the game you are playing is server-based, and the data, such as stats, achievements, or characters, is saved in the cloud. All the game needs to do is synchronise your and your friends actions with the server.

2020s

In the 2020s, technology will become more sophisticated but also stronger and better. Cloud gaming is sometimes called gaming on demand. Cloud gaming is a service that runs the video game on remote servers and streams it directly to a user's device. This allows the user to play games without the need for a high-end computer and to download large games. The first cloud gaming service started in 2003 but failed because of low bandwidth. Although cloud gaming today is much better, it will become even better with improvements.

When it comes to virtual reality or VR technology, which is goggles or headsets that create a simulated world that can be used for gaming as well as training or research, these simulation worlds are in 3D, which is able to follow head movements and incorporate auditory and visual feedback, making the VR experience realistic.

Similar to VR is AR (augmented reality). Augmented games are where the game takes a real-time video game image and renders additional graphics over it. A common game console that uses AR technology is the Xbox 360 and Xbox One games using Kinect (motion-sensing input devices that use rgb cameras and infrared projects and detectors to capture real-time gesture recognition and body skeletal detection). Another game that uses AR is Pokemon Go, released in 2016. to show off graphical creatures on a background using real-time video from your phone camera.

In 2020, one of the two biggest manufacturers of video graphics cards, Nvidia and AMD, introduced hardware support for real-time ray tracing technology on their latest GPUs. Ray tracing is a type of graphic technique that creates light transport from a range of rendering algorithms for generating digital images. This technology helps make light shadows, reflections, and refractions behave more smoothly. Have a look at his video comparison of ray tracing.

There is other technology that makes games unique with close-to-realistic graphics. There are many techniques to make games realistic. However, we will be going with only two techniques as there are too many. Spatial anti-aliasing is a technique that smooths out the edges seen in images while playing games or editing digital photos. This technique allows the blues to appear less blue and blends colours to make them look natural. You can also see sharp edges, mostly on old 80s games that have an 8-bit feel.

The last technology we will discuss is DLSS (deep learning supersampling fps). DLSS is an image enhancement and upscaling technology that works in real time with deep learning AI developed by Nvidia. DLSS helps boost the performance of your game without the need for a stronger GPU or computer. If you want to play 4K content but can only run 1080p at 60 fps, you can apply DLSS, which will upscale the resolution while maintaining the fps. DLSS can also improve your frame rate if the game you want to play struggles with 30 fps. The problem with DLSS is that you need games that are compatible with this technology and a decent PC, as it will still need processing power to cope with DLSS and the quirks of the game such as blur, ghosting, and bad aliasing. However, DLSS is still powerful and useful to this day.
